Research Interests

Our core research focuses on addressing critical challenges at the intersection of chemistry, physics, and modern materials engineering.

Battery Materials

Periodic DFT investigates novel 2D materials as Li/Na-ion battery anodes, revealing high capacity, stability, positive OCV, and fast diffusion.

Hydrogen Storage Materials

Hydrogen generated from solar water splitting can be reversibly stored in metal-functionalized MOFs and calixarenes for clean fuel-cell energy.

Dynamics of Ion-Neutral and Neutral-Neutral Collisions

Quantum studies of proton–molecule collisions provide reaction rates and energy-transfer data crucial for atmospheric and interstellar chemistry modeling.

Ultracold Collisions

Theoretical studies of molecule–helium collisions provide inelastic cross sections essential for buffer-gas cooling, ultracold trapping, spectroscopy, and astrophysics.
