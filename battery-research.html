Battery Materials

Energy storage methods are cardinal for the mitigation of intermittency of renewable energy. Among the many technologies available, metal-ion batteries—mainly Li-ion and Na-ion batteries—dominate the market on account of their significant attributes. However, there is still room for improvement in their performance for high-end applications. Since modifying the anode material can significantly alter the overall performance, discovering a satisfactory negative electrode is essential for developing more productive metal-ion batteries.

In this regard, the accelerated growth of two-dimensional (2D) materials with near-atomic thickness has paved the way for exploring these unique systems in the electrochemical energy storage field. Their exceptional properties—including a large surface area, great structural stability, flexibility, fast electron transfer speed, and tunable electronic behavior—render 2D materials key components and strong candidates for battery applications. Consequently, we investigate novel 2D materials for metal-ion battery anodes using Periodic Density Functional Theory (DFT).

To ensure structural and performance viability, the candidate materials are systematically investigated for their mechanical properties using the strain-energy method, thermal stability using Ab Initio Molecular Dynamics (AIMD), and dynamic stability using phonon spectrum analyses. Density of States (DOS) and band structure plots are implemented to evaluate electronic behaviors. Furthermore, the Nudged Elastic Band (NEB) method is used to determine the minimum energy path for Li migration over the Biphenylene monolayer, allowing us to calculate critical properties like open-circuit voltage (OCV) and storage capacity.

So far, our research group has successfully investigated Siligraphene, C-Silicyne, T-graphene, Si-doped T-graphene monolayers, β12-Borophene nanoribbons, and graphene-borophene heterostructures for their applications in Li-/Na-ion systems. Their low diffusion barriers and high storage capacities alongside positive OCVs render these materials highly promising. Our efforts continue to focus primarily on carbon-based materials, their doped analogues, and mono-elemental 2D sheets to explore their full potential in battery applications.

Core Methodologies
  • Periodic Density Functional Theory
  • Ab Initio Molecular Dynamics (AIMD)
  • Nudged Elastic Band (NEB) Path
  • Phonon Dispersion Frameworks
